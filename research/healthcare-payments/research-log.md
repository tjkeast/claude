## 2026-06-07 — Entry 001: HICAPS and the Australian healthcare payments landscape

**Focus:** Establish a baseline — what HICAPS is, its market position, the
competitive landscape, and the broader industry context.

### HICAPS — what it is
- **HICAPS** = **H**ealth **I**ndustry **C**laims **a**nd **P**ayments **S**ervice.
  An electronic claiming + payment system used at the point of care by doctors,
  dentists, and allied health providers.
- A **wholly owned subsidiary of National Australia Bank (NAB)**; established
  **1998**. It is a **closed-loop, market-dominant** system and a key reason NAB
  has historically been the biggest lender to healthcare providers.
- Scale: used by **100% of Australia's private health funds**, **~100,000+
  health service providers/practitioners** (one source cites 94,000+), and
  processes **~41 million claims per year**.
- Function: real-time processing of private health insurance claims at point of
  service, plus **EFTPOS**, **Medicare Easyclaim**, and **bulk billing**.

### Recent HICAPS/NAB moves (innovation)
- **Apple/Android Wallet:** Members of Medibank, Bupa, nib, and GU Health can add
  their health fund membership card to a digital wallet and make contactless
  claims at HICAPS terminals.
- **Best Practice integration (2025):** HICAPS integrated with Best Practice GP
  practice-management software (~6,000 clinics); NAB billed as the first major
  bank to do this type of GP-software integration.
- **OCR on terminals:** New terminals can convert an invoice/receipt image into
  machine-readable data.
- **Acquisition — LanternPay / Lantern Claims:** HICAPS agreed to acquire 100% of
  Lantern Claims (operator of LanternPay) from InLoop — a **bank-agnostic** claims
  rail (notably used by CBA and ANZ) — to enhance HICAPS' payment process and
  payment options. (Worth tracking: integration progress + competition angle.)

### Competitive landscape
- **Tyro Health (HealthPoint):** Tyro **acquired Medipass** from NAB Ventures for
  **A$22.5M**. Tyro **phased out HICAPS support on 3 Feb 2023** and pushed its own
  **HealthPoint** rail. Reach cited: ~42 integrated practice-management systems
  and ~9,300 health merchant customers. HealthPoint is effectively a mobile
  version of terminal-based health fund card processing.
- **LanternPay** and **DXC** built **bank-agnostic rails** for health
  payments/claims — used by **CBA and ANZ** (before NAB's move to buy LanternPay).
- **CommBank (CommBank Health / Smart Health):** offers payment solutions for
  health professionals — a major-bank competitor to HICAPS.
- Practice-management software ecosystems (Halaxy, Cliniko/HealthPoint, Nookal,
  Best Practice) are the **distribution battleground** — whoever integrates with
  the PMS owns the workflow.

### Broader industry context (Australia)
- **Australia digital health market:** ~**US$8.9B in 2025**, projected
  ~**US$31.1B by 2034** (~14.9% CAGR).
- **Australia healthcare services market:** ~**US$215.5B in 2025** → ~US$391.9B by
  2034 (~6.66% CAGR).
- **Medicare Easyclaim** is the digital channel for Medicare bulk-bill and patient
  claims; the Australian Government Dept. of Health is working to modernise the
  "assignment of benefit" / Medicare payment process for transparency + integrity.

### Global trends (2025) relevant to the space
- **Embedded finance / "card on file":** auto-charging patient co-pays after a
  visit with no patient action — the biggest structural trend.
- **Claims automation / RPA / AI** in the revenue cycle: auto-reconciliation,
  ERA file processing, denial management. CAQH estimates ~**US$25B/yr** potential
  provider savings from streamlining claims & payments.
- **Near real-time processing** becoming the expectation.
- **Rising claim denials:** insurers denied more claims on clinical grounds in
  2025 vs 2024 → ~25% increase in net revenue leakage at hospitals (Kodiak).
